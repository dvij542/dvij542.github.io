üî¨ Research

Broadly, I am interested in building general purpose robots that can interact safely with humans and can adapt to unknown scenarios. Currently, my research interests are in:

  • Marrying game theory with robotics enabling use of classical game theoretic approaches to analyze Nash-equilibrium in multi-agent collaboration with robots and humans
  • The use of alpha-potential games framework to obtain Nash equilibrium in competitive scenarios like autonomous multi-car racing, robotic ping pong
  • Enabling safe, robust and adaptive control for agile systems in unknown environments

Real-Time Algorithms for Game-Theoretic Motion Planning and Control in Autonomous Racing using Near-Potential Function.
Dvij Kalaria*, Chinmay Maheshwari*, Shankar Sastry
Under Review, L4DC 2025  

website | code, arxiv, social media coming soon!

PotentialRace: Formulates multi-car racing as an alpha-potential game that learns a potential function for parameterized set of policies offline. The potential function is optimized online to approximate Nash equilibrium in real-time.

Learning to Anticipate Table Tennis Hits from Monocular Video
Daniel Etaat, Dvij Kalaria, Nima Rahmanian, Shankar Sastry
Under Review, CVPR 2025

website, code, arxiv, dataset, social media coming soon!

A scalable system for reconstructing monocular videos of table tennis matches in 3D. This data is used to train a large transformer capable of anticipating opponent actions through conformal prediction. Reconstructed trajectories are simulated in MuJoCo with a robotic system on receiving end capable of returning balls with 61.1% accuracy as compared to 44.3% with no anticipation
